# LinkedIn Content Frameworks

Proven structures for different types of LinkedIn posts.

## Personal Experience Framework
**Best for**: Thought leadership, storytelling, lessons learned
**Structure**:
1. Hook (2 lines): Bold statement or intriguing question
2. Story Setup (2-3 paragraphs): Set the scene, create context
3. The Challenge (1-2 paragraphs): What went wrong or was difficult
4. The Turning Point (1-2 paragraphs): Insight, action, or realization
5. The Lesson (1-2 paragraphs): What you learned, how it applies broadly
6. CTA (1 line): Question to spark discussion

## Contrarian Take Framework
**Best for**: Thought leadership, sparking debate, standing out
**Structure**:
1. Contrarian Hook (1-2 lines): Challenge conventional wisdom
2. Common Belief (1 paragraph): What everyone thinks
3. Why It's Wrong (2-3 paragraphs): Evidence and reasoning
4. Better Approach (2-3 paragraphs): Your alternative perspective
5. Proof/Example (1-2 paragraphs): Real-world validation
6. CTA (1 line): Invite agreement or counterarguments

## Listicle Framework
**Best for**: Educational content, quick wins, shareability
**Structure**:
1. Hook (1-2 lines): Promise value ("X ways to Y")
2. Brief Context (1-2 sentences): Why this matters
3. List Items (5-7 items): Bold header + 2-3 sentence explanation
4. Summary (1 paragraph): Tie it together
5. CTA (1 line): Ask which tip they'll try first

## Problem-Agitate-Solve Framework
**Best for**: Product launches, selling services, lead generation
**Structure**:
1. Hook (1-2 lines): Identify the painful problem
2. Problem Deep-Dive (2-3 paragraphs): Show you understand the pain
3. Agitate (1-2 paragraphs): Make the problem feel urgent
4. Solution Intro (1 paragraph): Present your solution
5. How It Works (2-3 paragraphs): Explain the mechanism
6. Proof (1 paragraph): Results, testimonials, data
7. CTA (1 line): Clear next step

## Behind-the-Scenes Framework
**Best for**: Building connection, showing personality, humanizing brands
**Structure**:
1. Hook (1-2 lines): Tease the behind-the-scenes moment
2. The Scene (2-3 paragraphs): Paint the picture vividly
3. The Surprise/Insight (1-2 paragraphs): What wasn't expected
4. The Lesson (1-2 paragraphs): What it taught you
5. Application (1 paragraph): How others can apply this
6. CTA (1 line): Ask about their experience

## Data/Research Framework
**Best for**: Establishing credibility, educational content, thought leadership
**Structure**:
1. Hook (1-2 lines): Lead with surprising statistic
2. Context (1 paragraph): Where the data comes from
3. Deep Dive (3-4 paragraphs): What the data reveals + implications
4. Actionable Insights (2-3 paragraphs): What to do with this information
5. CTA (1 line): Ask for their take or experience

## How-To/Tutorial Framework
**Best for**: Educational content, building authority, providing value
**Structure**:
1. Hook (1-2 lines): Promise specific outcome
2. Why This Matters (1 paragraph): Context and motivation
3. Step-by-Step Process (4-6 steps): Clear numbering, action-oriented
4. Pro Tips (2-3 bullet points): Advanced insights
5. CTA (1 line): Encourage them to try it

## Transformation Story Framework
**Best for**: Inspiration, relatability, building connection
**Structure**:
1. Hook (1-2 lines): Tease the transformation
2. The Before (2-3 paragraphs): Where you started, the struggle
3. The Catalyst (1-2 paragraphs): What changed everything
4. The Journey (2-3 paragraphs): The process of change
5. The After (1-2 paragraphs): Current state, results
6. The Lesson (1 paragraph): What it taught you
7. CTA (1 line): Ask about their transformation
