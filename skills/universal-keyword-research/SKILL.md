# UNIVERSAL KEYWORD RESEARCH

A platform-agnostic keyword research methodology. Keyword research isn't a Pinterest thing or a blog thing: it's a "find the actual words real people use before writing anything" practice, and the same underlying process applies whether the deliverable is a pin, a blog post, an Etsy listing, an email subject line, a YouTube title, or an AI Overview answer block. This skill is the research methodology. It does not write the final content itself; it produces a validated, prioritized keyword list that feeds into whatever you write next.

## WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL

- Any explicit keyword research or keyword strategy request, on any platform
- Planning a content pillar, content calendar, or topic cluster, not just a single quick post
- Competitor gap analysis ("what are competitors covering that this site/shop isn't")
- Questions about specific keyword tools (Ahrefs, Semrush, Keysearch, Ubersuggest, Pinterest Trends, Google Keyword Planner, KeywordToPin, or others)
- AI search / answer engine optimization (AEO) keyword and query questions (query fan-out, AI Overview targeting, "what would ChatGPT or Perplexity surface for this")
- "What should I title, name, or tag this" for any product, post, or listing

## CORE WORKFLOW (APPLIES TO EVERY PLATFORM)

1. **Clarify the actual goal:** what is this keyword research for (a content pillar, a calendar, a single product, a gap check), and which platform(s) matter
2. **Generate seed keywords:** from the product or topic itself, stated in the words a customer or searcher would actually use, not internal/brand language (see `references/core-keyword-fundamentals.md` Section 1)
3. **Expand the seed list:** autocomplete, related-term tools, AI-assisted brainstorming. Treat this expanded list as a starting point only, never a finished one
4. **Tag search intent:** informational, navigational, commercial investigation, or transactional (Section 2 of the same file)
5. **Validate against real data:** autocomplete, an actual keyword tool, or platform-native trend data. Never hand over a brainstormed guess with the same confidence as verified data (Section 7)
6. **Score and prioritize:** relevance, volume, winnability, and business potential, each rated 1 to 5 (Section 6)
7. **Hand off the output:** to whatever content-creation step comes next (writing the post, building the pin, drafting the listing)

## A NOTE ON FACTS AND TOOLS

Every statistic and tool claim in the reference files was checked against a current, named source as of the `last_updated` date in this file's frontmatter, not pulled from memory or assumed current. Tool pricing specifically changes often and is flagged with a "confirm before quoting" note wherever it appears. Treat any number in this skill as directional and confirm it on the vendor's own site before it goes into a budget conversation or a client-facing document. If a tool comes up that isn't covered in these files, research it the same way before recommending it: confirm what it actually does and what it costs from a current source, rather than assuming from the name or from general category knowledge.

## OUTPUT FORMAT

A finished keyword research deliverable should include, at minimum:
- The seed keyword(s) and where they came from
- The validated keyword list, grouped by parent topic/cluster where relevant
- Search intent tagged per keyword
- A priority score, or at least a clear best-to-worst rank, per keyword
- A flag on anything still brainstormed-only and not yet validated against real data

## QUALITY CHECKLIST

Before treating a keyword research pass as finished:

- [ ] Every keyword traces back to a seed and a reason it was included
- [ ] Search intent is tagged for every keyword
- [ ] Brainstormed-but-unvalidated keywords are clearly flagged as such, not presented as confirmed data
- [ ] Tool and pricing claims are current and sourced, not assumed
- [ ] Priority or ranking is present, not just a flat list
- [ ] Numbers attributed to a named source (a specific study, a specific tool's own page) rather than presented as general knowledge
